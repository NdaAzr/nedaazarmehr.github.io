I am passionate about teaching and mentoring the next generation of researchers and practitioners in data science, artificial intelligence, and digital health. My teaching draws on both academic theory and real-world research experience.

Courses I Teach

  • INF6028 - Data Mining: Machine learning foundations, classification, clustering, deep learning.
  • INF6032 - Big Data Analytics: Distributed computing, data pipelines, and large-scale data processing using Spark and Python.

Student Supervision

I supervise MSc dissertations and welcome students who are interested in applying AI and data science to healthcare, medical imaging, and digital health challenges. I also co-supervise PhD students and enjoy interdisciplinary collaboration across engineering, clinical, and public health fields.

Teaching Philosophy

My teaching approach is student-centered and research-informed. I aim to equip students with both critical thinking and practical skills, encouraging creativity, curiosity, and responsible innovation in AI.
